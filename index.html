A Brief History of Poetry

  1. 2000 B.C. The first instance of Poetry appeared as early as the 20th century B.C.
  2. 1900 B.C. The Epic of Gilgamesh often is cited as one of the earliest works of epic poetry.
  3. 1300 Petrarca, for whom the Petrarchan sonnet is named, is perhaps one of the most famous early writers of the sonnet.
  4. 1500 It became best known as an English poetic form through the work of William Shakespeare
  5. 1600 Following the reign of Queen Elizabeth I, the English Restoration period (from 1660-1689) saw the rise of literary elites, such as John Dryden.
  6. 1800 In the Romantics and 19th-Century Poetry, some important poets to consider include key Romantic poets such as William Blake, Samuel Taylor Coleridge, William Wordsworth, and John Keats.
  7. 1900 Toward the turn of the 20th century as Whitman continued to revise his 1855 edition of Leaves of Grass, he wrote, “Of Modern Man I Sing,” ushering in a new period—and a variety of forms—for poetry.
  8. 1900 The 20th century also witnessed a number of poets winning the Nobel Prize, from the United States to India.
  9. Future The development of poetry is ongoing.
